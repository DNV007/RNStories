When Atoms Hug — A Water Drop Is Born!
Now listen carefully, Rohinoy — and Arushi, stop eating the book, please! 😂

Those two H atoms and that one big O atom? They zoom toward each other at great speed and — BAM! — they hug so tightly they become ONE thing. That thing is called a water molecule! And when MILLIONS of molecules hug together, they become a drop of water you can actually see!
It's like Rohinoy running to hug Arushi so hard they both fall on the sofa! 💥 Except atoms don't bonk their heads. Usually.
💧 1 drop = ~2 million trillion molecules!
🔗 The hug is called a "bond"
✨ It happens in a flash!

Water's Three Outfits: Solid, Liquid & Gas!
Here is something amazing, Rohinoy — water is like a superhero who has THREE different costumes!

🧊 ICE — when it's very cold, water molecules slow down and hold hands in a grid, standing very stiff. Like Arushi when she doesn't want to take a bath! 😄

💧 LIQUID — normal water — molecules wiggle and dance freely. Like you two after eating too much dessert!

☁️ GAS/VAPOR — water can slowly turn into invisible gas at ANY temperature (that's why wet clothes dry!). But at 100°C it boils fast and turns to gas very quickly. Like Grandpa's tea disappearing as steam!
Arushi once tried to catch the steam from Grandpa's tea. She grabbed at the air, then looked at her empty hands very confused. That's right, little one — it escaped!! 😂
🔬 Temperature controls the costume!
Below 0°C → ICE. Between 0°C and 100°C → LIQUID water. Water slowly turns to invisible gas (evaporation) at any temperature — but at 100°C it boils and turns to gas very fast! The same water molecules, just in different moods!

Why Does Ice Float? (It's Cheating, Sort Of!)
Now this is the SNEAKY part, Rohinoy. Listen!

When water becomes ice, the molecules hold hands in a special snowflake shape — hexagons! Six-sided rings, like tiny honeycombs. And these rings have gaps in the middle — empty spaces!

All those empty spaces mean ice takes up MORE room than the same water would. So ice is lighter — less dense — and floats on top of liquid water!

But here's the sneaky secret: only about 1/10th of the ice sticks up above the water. The other BIG 9/10ths hides underwater — like an iceberg hiding most of itself! That's why ships have to be very careful. 🚢

Ice is basically an iceberg in disguise! 😄
Arushi's rubber duck floats too — but for different reasons! The duck is full of air inside. Ice floats because of its sneaky hexagon shape. Both are showing off. 🦆🧊
🔬 This is VERY important for life!
Because ice floats, ponds and oceans freeze from the TOP down — not the bottom. The water underneath stays liquid, so fish survive! And here's a jaw-dropper: about 90% of floating ice hides below the water surface — only a tiny ~10% tip sticks out. That's what makes icebergs so dangerous for ships! 🚢 Good job, hexagons! 🎉

The Great Water Carousel! (Water Cycle)
Are you ready for the most magical story of all? Water never runs away. It goes on a giant round trip — over and over, forever!

☀️ The Sun warms the ocean and rivers — so water turns to invisible steam and floats UP into the sky!

🌥️ High up where it's cold, the steam turns back into tiny droplets — they become clouds!

🌧️ The clouds get heavy and — DRIP DRIP SPLASH — rain falls down!

🏔️ Rain runs down mountains into rivers… and BACK to the ocean. Then the whole ride starts again!

The same water has been doing this loop for BILLIONS of years!
Grandpa's theory: the water in Arushi's bath tonight? It might once have been drunk by a dinosaur! 🦕💧 Or rained down on ancient Egypt! Arushi is bathing in history! 😄
🔬 No new water is ever made on Earth!
Earth has had the same water for about 4.5 BILLION years. Every glass of water, every rain shower, every ocean wave — it's all ancient water recycling through the same loop. You and the dinosaurs drank from the same water! 🦕🥤

The Kettle, the Steam, and the Super Machine!
Last chapter! You both did so well! Grandpa is so proud! 🥹

You know Grandpa's kettle in the kitchen? When water gets to 100°C, it boils and turns to steam. Right near the spout, the steam is invisible — you can't see it yet! A little farther away it cools slightly and you see the white fluffy cloud.

Now here's the clever part — long ago, clever people thought: "What if we TRAP that steam inside a big metal box?" The steam pushes really hard. It pushes a piston back and forth. That piston turns a wheel. And that wheel can pull a whole train! 🚂

Steam engines changed the whole world! All because of boiling water!
Grandpa once stared at the kettle steam for SO long trying to understand it, he forgot the tea was done and it went cold. Grandpa had to heat it up again. Steam: 1, Grandpa: 0. ☕😂
🔬 From kettle to trains, ships, factories!
Steam engines powered the Industrial Revolution! Big ships, coal mines, cotton factories — all ran on boiling water's power. So next time your kettle whistles, Rohinoy, remember: the same idea once pulled a 200-tonne train! 🚂💨
